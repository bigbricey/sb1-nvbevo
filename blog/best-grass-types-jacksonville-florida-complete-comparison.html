Best Grass Types for Jacksonville, Florida: Complete Comparison Guide

Published: January 20, 2024

Quick Answer

The best grass type for most Jacksonville lawns is St. Augustine (particularly Floratam variety) due to its heat tolerance, shade adaptability, and attractive appearance. For high-traffic areas, Bermuda grass is ideal. Zoysia offers excellent drought resistance with moderate shade tolerance. For low-maintenance options, Bahia or Centipede grass work well in Jacksonville's climate, though each has specific advantages and limitations based on your yard's unique conditions.

Understanding Jacksonville's Unique Growing Environment

Selecting the right grass type for your Jacksonville lawn requires understanding our local growing conditions, which create unique challenges and opportunities. As Northeast Florida lawn care specialists, we've installed thousands of lawns and observed what thrives in our specific environment.

Jacksonville's climate and soil conditions include:

  • USDA Hardiness Zones: 8b-9a, with mild winters and occasional frost
  • Annual Rainfall: Approximately 52 inches, with summer concentration
  • Soil Type: Predominantly sandy soil with rapid drainage and poor nutrient retention
  • Temperature Range: Hot, humid summers (90°F+) and mild winters (occasional dips below freezing)
  • Growing Season: Extended (March-November), allowing significant growth for warm-season grasses
  • Common Stressors: Periods of drought, occasional cold snaps, high pest pressure, and fungal disease due to humidity

These conditions favor warm-season grasses, but each variety has different strengths and weaknesses that make it more or less suitable for specific yard situations. This comprehensive comparison will help you identify the ideal grass type for your unique Jacksonville property.

Local Insight

In Jacksonville, over 70% of residential lawns use St. Augustine grass varieties (primarily Floratam), while Bermuda is commonly seen on athletic fields and golf courses. Zoysia has been gaining popularity in recent years for residential properties seeking enhanced drought tolerance with good appearance.

St. Augustine Grass (Stenotaphrum secundatum)

St. Augustine grass is the most popular choice for Jacksonville lawns, and for good reason. This grass type has adapted well to our region's climate, sandy soils, and provides an attractive, lush appearance with good shade tolerance.

Pros

  • Excellent heat tolerance for Jacksonville's hot summers
  • Good shade adaptation (best among warm-season grasses)
  • Forms dense, carpet-like turf that crowds out weeds
  • Handles Jacksonville's sandy soils well
  • Good salt tolerance for coastal properties
  • Attractive blue-green color
  • Establishes quickly from sod

Cons

  • Susceptible to chinch bugs, especially during dry periods
  • Less traffic-tolerant than Bermuda or Zoysia
  • Requires regular irrigation during drought
  • Vulnerable to certain fungal diseases in high humidity
  • Can be damaged by prolonged frost
  • Higher fertilizer requirements than some alternatives
  • Limited cold tolerance

Popular St. Augustine Varieties for Jacksonville

Several St. Augustine varieties perform well in Jacksonville, each with slightly different characteristics:

  • Floratam: The most common variety in Jacksonville, with good chinch bug resistance, heat tolerance, and moderate shade tolerance (up to 5-6 hours of direct sunlight needed)
  • Palmetto: Enhanced shade tolerance (can thrive with 4+ hours of sunlight), finer texture, and good cold tolerance
  • Seville: Superior shade tolerance (can grow with only 3+ hours of sunlight), but somewhat less drought tolerant
  • Bitterblue: Darker blue-green color, dense growth, good shade tolerance, but more susceptible to chinch bugs
  • CitraBlue: Newer variety with improved disease resistance, drought tolerance, and shade tolerance

Ideal Conditions for St. Augustine in Jacksonville

St. Augustine thrives under these Jacksonville conditions:

  • Partial to full sun (4-6+ hours of direct sunlight daily, depending on variety)
  • Irrigation available during dry periods
  • Moderate foot traffic areas
  • Regular maintenance schedule (mowing, fertilization, pest monitoring)
  • Clay-loam to sandy soils

Maintenance Tip

For St. Augustine lawns in Jacksonville, maintain a mowing height of 3.5-4 inches during summer and monitor regularly for signs of chinch bug activity (patches of yellowing/dying grass, especially in sunny areas). Apply iron supplements rather than excess nitrogen when a greener appearance is desired during the growing season.

Cost Considerations

St. Augustine sod typically costs $0.50-$0.85 per square foot in the Jacksonville area, depending on the variety and quantity. While more expensive than some alternatives, its adaptability to local conditions often makes it a cost-effective long-term choice.

Bermuda Grass (Cynodon dactylon)

Bermuda grass is prized for its exceptional durability, drought tolerance, and rapid recovery from damage. In Jacksonville, it's commonly used for athletic fields, golf courses, and high-traffic residential areas where its resilience outweighs its higher maintenance requirements.

Pros

  • Superior wear tolerance for active families with children, pets
  • Excellent heat and drought resistance
  • Very rapid recovery from damage
  • Fine texture creates manicured appearance when properly maintained
  • Quick establishment and growth rate
  • Good cold tolerance for Jacksonville's occasional freezes
  • High disease resistance

Cons

  • Poor shade tolerance (requires 8+ hours of direct sunlight)
  • High maintenance (frequent mowing needed during peak growth)
  • Aggressive growth can invade flower beds and gardens
  • More difficult to eradicate if replacement is desired
  • Tends to turn brown and go dormant in winter
  • Can develop thatch issues if not managed properly
  • Requires more frequent mowing (every 5-7 days in summer)

Popular Bermuda Varieties for Jacksonville

Several improved Bermuda varieties perform well in Northeast Florida:

  • Celebration: Improved drought tolerance, better shade tolerance than common Bermuda, excellent recuperative ability
  • Tifway 419: Fine texture, high density, excellent wear tolerance, commonly used on athletic fields
  • TifTuf: Enhanced drought resistance, good cold tolerance, maintains color longer in fall
  • Latitude 36: Improved cold tolerance, fine texture, good shade tolerance for a Bermuda
  • Princess 77: Very fine texture, dark green color, high density

Ideal Conditions for Bermuda in Jacksonville

Bermuda grass performs best under these circumstances:

  • Full sun (minimum 8 hours direct sunlight daily)
  • High-traffic areas that need durability
  • Well-drained soils
  • Households willing to commit to regular maintenance
  • Areas where winter dormancy (browning) is acceptable
  • Properties without nearby garden beds that could be invaded

Important Consideration

While Bermuda's aggressive growth habit makes it excellent for recovery, it can also become invasive to garden beds, pavers, and neighboring lawns. Consider using physical barriers like edging that extends 6+ inches below the soil surface to contain it effectively.

Cost Considerations

Bermuda sod typically costs $0.45-$0.65 per square foot in Jacksonville, with hybrid varieties on the higher end. While the initial cost may be lower than St. Augustine, the higher maintenance requirements (more frequent mowing, potential for more weed control) should be factored into long-term cost evaluations.

Zoysia Grass (Zoysia spp.)

Zoysia grass has been gaining popularity in Jacksonville as a middle-ground option that balances the durability of Bermuda with better shade tolerance, while offering improved drought resistance compared to St. Augustine. It creates a dense, carpet-like lawn with excellent weed suppression.

Pros

  • Excellent drought tolerance once established
  • Good traffic tolerance and recovery
  • Moderate shade tolerance (better than Bermuda)
  • Dense growth habit that suppresses weeds
  • Fine to medium texture with excellent feel underfoot
  • Lower fertilizer requirements than St. Augustine or Bermuda
  • Good cold tolerance for Jacksonville winters

Cons

  • Slow establishment period (can take 1-2 seasons to fully establish)
  • Slower to recover from damage than Bermuda
  • Goes dormant (brown) in winter sooner than St. Augustine
  • Can develop thatch if not properly maintained
  • More expensive sod than other options
  • Less shade tolerant than St. Augustine
  • Can be difficult to mow with rotary mowers

Popular Zoysia Varieties for Jacksonville

Several Zoysia varieties perform well in Northeast Florida:

  • Empire: Medium-bladed, excellent drought tolerance, good shade performance, and low maintenance requirements; the most common Zoysia variety in Florida
  • Zeon: Fine-bladed with excellent shade tolerance, forms a dense carpet with good wear resistance
  • JaMur: Quick establishment for a Zoysia, good cold tolerance, moderate shade tolerance
  • Geo: Fine-textured with excellent density and wear tolerance
  • Zorro: Very fine-bladed with superior shade tolerance and excellent visual appeal

Ideal Conditions for Zoysia in Jacksonville

Zoysia performs best in these Jacksonville conditions:

  • Full sun to partial shade (6+ hours direct sunlight preferred)
  • Areas with water restrictions or limited irrigation
  • Moderate to high traffic areas
  • When a balance between appearance and maintenance is desired
  • Properties where winter dormancy is acceptable
  • When long-term performance is valued over quick establishment

Establishment Tip

Zoysia requires patience during establishment. For the first 2-3 weeks after installation, water new Zoysia sod according to normal establishment guidelines. However, unlike Bermuda or St. Augustine which establish quickly, Zoysia may take an entire growing season to fully root and reach its potential. Regular irrigation during this extended establishment period is crucial.

Cost Considerations

Zoysia sod typically costs $0.60-$0.85 per square foot in Jacksonville, making it one of the more expensive grass options. However, its reduced maintenance needs (less water, fertilizer, and mowing than some alternatives) can offset the higher initial investment over time.

Bahia Grass (Paspalum notatum)

Bahia grass is known for its exceptional drought resistance and low maintenance requirements. While less manicured in appearance than other options, it offers unmatched resilience in challenging conditions and is often used for larger properties, roadside areas, and locations where minimal maintenance is desired.

Pros

  • Superior drought tolerance with deep root system
  • Minimal fertilization requirements
  • Excellent adaptability to poor, sandy soils
  • Good insect and disease resistance
  • Low maintenance needs overall
  • Can be established from seed (unlike St. Augustine)
  • Good cold tolerance for Jacksonville winters

Cons

  • Coarse texture and less attractive appearance
  • Produces tall seedheads that require frequent mowing in summer
  • Limited shade tolerance
  • Slower to establish than some alternatives
  • Less dense growth allows some weed penetration
  • Not ideal for formal or high-end landscapes
  • Difficult to remove if changing grass types later

Popular Bahia Varieties for Jacksonville

Several Bahia varieties are suitable for Northeast Florida:

  • Pensacola: The most common variety in Florida, with good cold tolerance and adaptability to poor soils
  • Argentine: Darker green, denser growth, and fewer seedheads than Pensacola, but less cold tolerant
  • Tifton-9: Improved vigor and density compared to Pensacola
  • UF-Riata: Enhanced cold tolerance and earlier spring green-up

Ideal Conditions for Bahia in Jacksonville

Bahia grass is best suited for:

  • Full sun locations (minimal shade tolerance)
  • Large properties where maintenance costs are a concern
  • Areas with no irrigation systems or limited water availability
  • Poor soil conditions where other grasses struggle
  • Low-input landscapes where functionality outweighs appearance
  • Properties where seedheads are not considered objectionable

Appearance Consideration

Bahia produces distinctive Y-shaped seedheads that can reach 2-3 feet in height if not mowed regularly during the growing season. While this is a natural characteristic of the grass, many homeowners find these seedheads objectionable in formal residential landscapes. For manicured properties, Bahia may not be the best choice despite its other advantages.

Cost Considerations

Bahia sod typically costs $0.30-$0.45 per square foot in Jacksonville, making it the most economical sod option. It can also be established from seed at even lower cost ($0.05-$0.10 per square foot), though establishment takes longer. The low maintenance requirements add to its cost-effectiveness for large areas.

Centipede Grass (Eremochloa ophiuroides)

Centipede grass is often called "the lazy man's grass" due to its low maintenance requirements and slow growth habit. In Jacksonville, it offers a middle ground between the refined appearance of St. Augustine and the low maintenance of Bahia, making it suitable for homeowners seeking reduced lawn care requirements.

Pros

  • Very low fertility requirements
  • Slow growth means less frequent mowing
  • Good heat tolerance for Jacksonville summers
  • Moderate shade tolerance
  • Forms reasonably dense turf that limits weeds
  • Tolerates acidic soils common in parts of Jacksonville
  • Lower maintenance than St. Augustine or Bermuda

Cons

  • Poor traffic tolerance and slow recovery from damage
  • Limited cold tolerance (can suffer in hard freezes)
  • Poor salt tolerance (not ideal for coastal properties)
  • Susceptible to iron deficiency in high-pH soils
  • Prone to thatch buildup over time
  • Vulnerable to nematode damage in sandy soils
  • Light green color may be considered less attractive

Popular Centipede Varieties for Jacksonville

While Centipede has fewer varieties than other grass types, these perform well in Northeast Florida:

  • Common Centipede: The standard variety with apple-green color and low maintenance needs
  • TifBlair: Improved cold tolerance and faster establishment than common Centipede
  • Centennial: Somewhat finer texture than common Centipede

Ideal Conditions for Centipede in Jacksonville

Centipede grass is best suited for:

  • Low-traffic residential lawns
  • Areas with acidic soils (pH 5.0-6.0)
  • Locations with moderate shade
  • Homeowners desiring minimal maintenance
  • Properties where a uniform appearance is desired without high inputs
  • Non-coastal areas (limited salt tolerance)

Centipede Decline Warning

Centipede grass can suffer from "centipede decline" if overfertilized or maintained at improper pH. In Jacksonville, maintain centipede at a pH of 5.0-6.0 and use no more than 1-2 pounds of nitrogen per 1,000 square feet annually. More is not better with this grass type!

Cost Considerations

Centipede sod typically costs $0.35-$0.55 per square foot in Jacksonville. While moderately priced for initial installation, its slow growth means replacement of damaged areas can be challenging. However, the reduced maintenance costs (less fertilizer, less frequent mowing) make it economical long-term.

Making the Right Choice for Your Jacksonville Property

Selecting the ideal grass type for your specific property depends on several key factors. Consider these questions to narrow down your options:

Key Questions to Consider

  1. How much direct sunlight does your yard receive?
    • 8+ hours: Any grass type will work (Bermuda optimal)
    • 6-8 hours: St. Augustine, Zoysia, or Bahia recommended
    • 4-6 hours: St. Augustine varieties (especially Palmetto or Seville)
    • Less than 4 hours: Consider alternatives to turfgrass (groundcovers, mulch, or shade-tolerant landscaping)
  2. How much foot traffic will the lawn receive?
    • Heavy (kids, pets, frequent use): Bermuda or Zoysia
    • Moderate: St. Augustine or Bahia
    • Light: Centipede or any other type
  3. How much maintenance are you willing to perform?
    • High (weekly mowing, regular fertilization): Bermuda, St. Augustine
    • Moderate: Zoysia, St. Augustine
    • Low: Centipede, Bahia
  4. Is irrigation available?
    • Full irrigation system: Any grass type suitable
    • Limited irrigation: Zoysia, Bahia
    • No irrigation: Bahia only
  5. Are you in a coastal area with salt exposure?
    • Yes: St. Augustine or Zoysia
    • No: Any grass type suitable
  6. How important is year-round green color?
    • Very important: St. Augustine (most cold-hardy)
    • Somewhat important: Zoysia
    • Not important: Bermuda, Bahia, or Centipede

Transitioning Between Grass Types

Switching from one grass type to another requires thorough removal of the existing grass. Here's how to approach common transitions:

  • Bermuda to St. Augustine or Zoysia: Most challenging transition due to Bermuda's persistence. Complete removal of all Bermuda stolons and rhizomes is necessary, often requiring multiple herbicide applications or physical removal with sod cutter set to remove 2-3 inches of soil.
  • St. Augustine to Zoysia or Bermuda: Relatively straightforward. Remove existing St. Augustine with a sod cutter, prepare soil, and install new sod.
  • Bahia to Other Types: Remove by sod cutter, but be vigilant for regrowth from deep rhizomes.
  • Centipede to Other Types: Can be removed with standard sod cutter and rarely presents regrowth issues.

Important Transition Note

Complete removal of the existing grass is crucial for successful transitions. Remnants of aggressive grasses like Bermuda will compete with and eventually invade the new lawn. In most cases, a professional installation with proper site preparation is recommended when changing grass types.

Conclusion: Selecting the Right Grass for Your Jacksonville Lawn

Choosing the perfect grass type for your Jacksonville property requires balancing several factors: your yard's growing conditions, your aesthetic preferences, maintenance capacity, and budget considerations. While St. Augustine remains the most popular choice for good reason, each grass type has situations where it excels.

For most Jacksonville homeowners, we recommend:

  • St. Augustine (Floratam): For typical suburban yards with mixed sun and shade conditions
  • Bermuda: For heavily used, full-sun yards where durability is the top priority
  • Zoysia: For homeowners seeking a balance of appearance, drought tolerance, and moderate maintenance
  • Bahia: For large properties, areas without irrigation, or locations where minimal maintenance is required
  • Centipede: For homeowners seeking a relatively attractive lawn with minimal inputs
